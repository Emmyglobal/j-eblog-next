Two Russian FSB Agents Killed After Assassinating Ukraine Spy Colonel

Published on July 13, 2025 • By Emma Marsden | Newsweek World

Ukraine’s Security Service (SBU) has confirmed the elimination of two Russian Federal Security Service (FSB) agents suspected of assassinating Colonel Ivan Voronych, a senior officer of the SBU’s Special Operations Centre.

The operation occurred just days after Voronych was gunned down in Kyiv on July 10. Surveillance footage showed the suspects trailing the colonel before ambushing him with a silenced pistol in the Holosiivskyi district.

SBU chief Vasyl Malyuk stated that the two FSB agents—one male and one female—entered Ukraine several days prior to the killing. They retrieved the murder weapon from a hidden arms cache before carrying out the assassination at around 9 a.m.

“The SBU eliminated the killers who killed the Security Service colonel in Kyiv,” read a statement from the Ukrainian government’s Telegram channel.

A special operation was launched in Kyiv Oblast to find the suspects. Upon locating them, the agents reportedly resisted arrest, leading to a shootout in which both were killed. The SBU emphasized that the mission was led personally by Lieutenant General Malyuk.

The Ukrainian Office of the Prosecutor General oversaw the legal and procedural elements of the operation, ensuring full accountability.

Malyuk praised the Ukrainian National Police for their role in the operation, highlighting that counterintelligence and the neutralization of Russian special operations groups remain a top priority.
