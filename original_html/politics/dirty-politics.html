The Dirty Game of Nigerian Politics – A System That Rewards the Ruthless

By Orbittech Blog•July 14, 2025

"Power tends to corrupt, and absolute power corrupts absolutely."

In Nigeria, that quote isn’t just poetic—it’s real. Ufuoma Bernard recently called Nigerian politics a dirty game, and few Nigerians could disagree. It’s a place where integrity is punished, and ruthlessness is rewarded.

Why Is the System Broken?

Because the structure itself discourages honesty. To win elections, politicians often rely on ballot-snatching, bribery, lies, violence, and godfathers. By the time an “honest man” makes it to the top, he’s either compromised—or he’s been silenced.

The Promise and the Betrayal

Buhari once symbolized hope. He promised to scrap the First Lady's office, fly commercial, remove fuel subsidies, and end medical tourism. None of these promises were kept. Nigerians were left heartbroken.

Can Good People Survive Politics?

Many believe they can. But the reality is: if you don’t play dirty, the system blocks your path. In 2023, some Labour Party candidates switched sides immediately after winning elections. Loyalty means nothing. Stomach infrastructure is everything.

Conclusion: Should We Lose Hope?

No. But we must face the truth. Nigerian politics isn’t built for saints. It’s a battlefield of betrayal, a theatre of greed. If the system doesn’t change, neither will the outcome.

So the question is: Do you still want to run for office?
