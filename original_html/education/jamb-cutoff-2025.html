JAMB Cut-Off Marks for 2025 Admissions

JAMB has pegged 150 as a cut-off mark for admission into universities. The board also fixed 100 as the minimum cut-off point mark for admission into polytechnics and colleges of education, while admission into Colleges of Nursing Sciences was fixed at 140.

JAMB Registrar, Professor Ishaq Oloyede announced this on Tuesday in Abuja at the 2025 JAMB Policy meeting.

Candidates are to note that the cut-off marks above are the JAMB minimum cut-off marks. This means institutions are free to set their own cut-off marks above the minimum but not below.

The meeting had in attendance the Minister of Education, Tunji Alaussa, vice-chancellors, rectors, registrars of higher institutions, and other key stakeholders.

During the meeting, it was also re-emphasised that the admissible age to tertiary institutions in Nigeria remains 16 years.

With the announcement of the cutoff marks, most public tertiary institutions will commence sales of Post-UTME screening forms in earnest. Candidates are advised to stay alert for announcements and updates from their institutions of choice.
